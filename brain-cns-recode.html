Brain/CNS Recode

SEER Brain and Other Central Nervous System tumor recode — based on the 2016 WHO Classification of Tumors of the Central Nervous System. All primary sites C700–C729 (brain/CNS) or C753 (pineal region). Source: seer.cancer.gov/seerstat/variables/seer/brain_cns-recode/

Purpose: Allows researchers to analyze brain and CNS tumors according to major WHO 2016 histological categories. Both malignant (behavior 3) and non-malignant (behavior 0 and 1) tumors are included — unlike most SEER recodes which capture malignant cases only.

Site scope: All rows apply to primary site codes C700–C729 (brain, spinal cord, and other CNS sites) unless otherwise noted. Pineal region tumors use site C753.

Recode 27 "All Other Cancer Types" captures any brain/CNS site record that does not meet criteria for recodes 01–26. This includes histologies excluded from the CNS recode (9050–9055, 9140, 9590–9993) as well as records at CNS sites with non-CNS tumor types (e.g. lymphomas, which are classified under the SEER Site Recode instead).

Reference: Forjaz G et al. An updated histology recode for the analysis of primary malignant and nonmalignant brain and other CNS tumors in SEER. Neurooncol Adv. 2020;3(1):vdaa175.

All Other Cancer Types

Recode 27 — All combinations not meeting criteria for recodes 01–26

Category Description Recode
All Other Cancer Types
Any record that does not meet the criteria for recodes 01–26. Includes:
  • CNS site records (C700–C729, C753) with excluded histologies (9050–9055 mesothelioma; 9140 Kaposi; 9590–9993 lymphomas and hematopoietic)
  • Non-CNS primary sites — these records will be classified by other SEER recodes (site recode, lymphoma subtype recode, etc.)
When restricting analyses to Brain/CNS tumors, filter to recodes 01–26 and exclude recode 27.
27
* Based on the 2016 WHO Classification of Tumors of the Central Nervous System.
ONS = Other Nervous System. NOS = Not Otherwise Specified. ATRT = Atypical Teratoid/Rhabdoid Tumor. PNET = Primitive Neuroectodermal Tumor. SEGA = Subependymal Giant Cell Astrocytoma. DNT = Dysembryoplastic Neuroepithelial Tumor.
Behavior codes: 0 = Benign; 1 = Borderline malignancy / uncertain behavior; 3 = Malignant.

Source: seer.cancer.gov/seerstat/variables/seer/brain_cns-recode/ — 2020 revision. Reference: Forjaz G et al. Neurooncol Adv. 2020;3(1):vdaa175.
