ICCC Recode ICD-O-3/WHO 2008

International Classification of Childhood Cancer — Main & Extended classification definitions based on ICD-O-3 site, histology, and behavior. Source: seer.cancer.gov/iccc/iccc-who2008.html

Key differences from the adult SEER Site Recode: The ICCC classifies cancer by morphology first, not primary site. The same histology code may appear in multiple groups depending on site. Two recode values are provided: the Extended Classification (3-digit, 001–114) for detailed subgroup analysis, and the Main Classification (3-digit, 011–122) for broader groupings.

Behavior codes: Most groups restrict to malignant behavior (ICD-O-3 behavior = 3). CNS and germ cell tumor groups include benign and borderline tumors (behavior 0 and 1) — flagged with a green banner. This reflects the clinical significance of intracranial tumors regardless of behavior.

Updated for WHO 2008: Hematopoietic codes updated per WHO Classification of Tumours of Haematopoietic and Lymphoid Tissues (2008). Subject to change based on evolving ICD-O-3 coding rules.
⚠ DENOMINATOR NOTE — Restrict population at risk to ages 0–19 when calculating ICCC-based rates
