Modifiable Risk Factor-Associated Cancers

ICD-O-3 site and histology codes defining cancer types associated with five modifiable risk factors. Source: CDC / U.S. Cancer Statistics Public Use Database — updated May 29, 2025.

Purpose: Cancer registries do not collect risk factor exposure data. These definitions identify cancer types linked to each risk factor by conclusive epidemiological evidence, enabling population-based burden estimates and cross-state comparisons. Rates calculated using these definitions can help identify high-burden communities and track the effectiveness of prevention efforts.

Note: Cancers appearing under multiple risk factors share the same ICD-O-3 codes across those definitions. The cross-reference matrix below shows which cancers appear under which risk factors. Further restrictions beyond those listed here may be appropriate depending on the analysis — see the SEER*Stat tutorial on merged variables.

HPV-Associated Cancers

Cancers causally linked to human papillomavirus (HPV) infection

Evidence basis: IARC Monographs Vol. 90 (2007); Watson et al. 2008; Saraiya et al. 2015; Viens et al. 2016
~1.8% of US cancer cases (HPV alone)
All Rows Have Notable Restrictions Every HPV-associated cancer definition uses a narrow histology range (squamous cell only, 8050–8086 & 8120–8131) and requires microscopic confirmation. Cervical carcinoma uses a broader histology range (8010–8671, 8940–8941). See full table below.

7.6% of US cancer cases
